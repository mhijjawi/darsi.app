👁️ How Do We See Objects?
💡
How Light Lets Us See
Light reflects off objects into your eyes

Imagine you're reading a book with a lamp on. The lamp sends out light. That light hits the book and bounces back (reflects) into your eyes. That's how you see things that don't glow on their own!

When light hits any object, 3 things can happen: it can bounce back (reflect), be soaked up (absorbed), or bend through (refract). Often all three happen at once!

🪨 Opaque Objects & Shadows
🪨
Opaque
Light CANNOT pass through → Shadow forms!

Opaque objects block ALL light. They only reflect or absorb it. Because no light gets through, a dark shape called a shadow forms on the other side!

Your body is opaque. That's why you cast a shadow on a sunny day. Thick wood, aluminum foil, and cardboard are all opaque.

🪟 Transparent Objects
🪟
Transparent
Light passes through FULLY → you see clearly

Transparent objects let ALL the light pass straight through them. You can see what's on the other side perfectly clearly. No shadow is formed because light passes right through!

🌫️ Translucent Objects
🌫️
Translucent
SOME light passes through → things look blurry

Translucent objects are in between opaque and transparent. They let some light through but block the rest. The light that gets through is scattered, making things on the other side look blurry.

☀️ Staying Safe in Sunlight
☀️
Sun Safety
Protect your eyes and skin from the sun

Sunlight can harm your eyes and cause sunburn on your skin. Always protect yourself on hot, sunny days! There are 3 great ways to stay safe:

🕶️
Sunglasses
Protect your eyes from harmful sunlight rays. Never look directly at the sun!
🧴
Sunscreen
Apply on your skin before going outside. Prevents painful sunburns!
🧢
Hat & Shade
A hat covers your head and face. Sitting in the shade also keeps you cool and safe!
🌈 Colors of Light — Did You Know?
🌈
Colors of Light
Sunlight is a mix of ALL colors!

Sunlight looks white, but it's actually a mixture of ALL the colors of the rainbow! When light hits an object, the object absorbs some colors and reflects others. The color you see is the one that bounces back to your eyes!
